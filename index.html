Biography

Diogo Maicon Krevoniz Balduino holds a master's degree in Applied Linguistics (2022) from the Interdisciplinary Graduate Program in Applied Linguistics (PIPGLA) at the Federal University of Rio de Janeiro (UFRJ/Capes). He also holds a BA in Languages and Literature with a specialization in English (2019) from the University of Vale do Rio dos Sinos (UNISINOS/ProUni). In 2022, he was awarded a Fulbright grant. He served as an Teaching Assistant of Portuguese Language at Washington and Lee University in the United States. He taught English to different age groups, including Elementary, Middle, and High School. Additionally, Diogo has taught at language programs, such as the Access E2C Program, which is sponsored by the US Embassy in Brazil. Understanding education as a political act, his teaching practice is committed to promoting critical and citizen linguistic education. In the classroom, he aims to create a welcoming space for dialogue and affection, engaged with diversity and social transformations. His current research interests include public health promotion policies and discourses on methods of sexually transmitted infection prevention, construction of identities, genders, and sexualities from interdisciplinary perspectives that establish dialogue between Applied Linguistics, Linguistic Anthropology, Medical Anthropology, Interactional Sociolinguistics, Conversation Analysis, and Queer Linguistics.
